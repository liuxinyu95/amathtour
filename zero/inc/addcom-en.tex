\section{Proof of commutativity of addition}
\phantomsection
\label[appendix]{app:add-commutativity}

\begin{proof}
To prove the commutativity of addition $a + b = b + a$, we prepare three things. The first states that, for any natural number $a$,

\be
0 + a = a
\label{eq:left-zero}
\ee

When $a = 0$, by the first rule of addition,

\[
0 + 0 = 0
\]

for induction, assume $0 + a = a$ holds, we are going to show $0 + a' = a'$.

\begin{align*}
0 + a' & = (0 + a)' && \text{second rule of addition} \\
       & = a'       && \text{induction assumption}
\end{align*}

Next we define the successor of 0 be 1, that is, $0' = 1$, then show,

\be
a' = a + 1
\label{eq:one-succ}
\ee

It means adding a number by 1 gives its successor. This is because:

\begin{align*}
a' & = (a + 0)' && \text{first rule of addition} \\
   & = a + 0'   && \text{second rule of addition} \\
   & = a + 1    && \text{1 is the successor of 0}
\end{align*}

The third thing we are going to show is,

\be
a + 1 = 1 + a
\label{eq:one-commu}
\ee

By induction on $a$, when $a = 0$,

\begin{align*}
0 + 1 & = 1     && \text{by \cref{eq:left-zero}} \\
      & = 1 + 0 && \text{first rule of addition}
\end{align*}

For induction, assume $a + 1 = 1 + a$ holds, we are going to show $a' + 1 = 1 + a'$.

\begin{align*}
a' + 1 & = a' + 0'        && \text{1 is the successor of 0} \\
       & = (a' + 0)'      && \text{first rule of addition} \\
       & = ((a + 1) + 0)' && \text{second result we proved. \cref{eq:one-succ}} \\
       & = (a + 1)'       && \text{second rule of addition} \\
       & = (1 + a)'       && \text{induction assumption} \\
       & = 1 + a'         && \text{second rule of addition}
\end{align*}

From these three results, we next prove the commutativity of addition. We first show that when $b = 0$ it holds. According to the first rule of addition, $a + 0 = a$; while by \cref{eq:left-zero}, $0 + a = a$, hence $a + 0 = 0 + a$. For induction case, assume $a + b = b + a$ holds, we are going to show $a + b' = b' + a$.

\begin{align*}
a + b' & = (a + b)' && \text{second rule of addition} \\
       & = (b + a)' && \text{induction assumption} \\
       & = b + a'   && \text{second rule of addition} \\
       & = b + a + 1 && \text{by \cref{eq:one-succ}} \\
       & = b + 1 + a && \text{by \cref{eq:one-commu}} \\
       & = (b + 1) + a && \text{associativity proven in \cref{sec:add-assoc}} \\
       & = b' + a  && \text{by \cref{eq:one-succ}}
\end{align*}

It proves the commutativity of addition with Peano's axioms\citepage[p147-148]{StepanovRose15}.

\end{proof}
